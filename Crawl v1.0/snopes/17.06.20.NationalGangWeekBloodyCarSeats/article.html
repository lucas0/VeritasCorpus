These warnings carried all the hallmarks of numerous other gang-related urban legends:


  • Gangs throughout the U.S. participate in some sort of organized nationwide scheme to engage in mayhem during a designated time period (e.g., the “ Halloween gang initiation” legend).

  • Gangs regularly target random, innocent victims for murder, typically as part of an initiation scheme for new members (e.g., the “ flashing headlights ” legend).

  • Gangs engage in a variety of schemes to lure random motorists out of their cars in order to beat, rob, or kill them (e.g., placing tire-puncturing spikes in shopping mall parking lots, affixing plastic baskets to the undersides of targeted vehicles, festooning cars’ windshields with flyers , acting as if they were drunk or had been struck by other cars, or hurling bricks or other objects at automobiles).

  • Gangs particularly target random, innocent (usually white) women (e.g., WalMart shoppers and gas station customers).
